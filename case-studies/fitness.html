the problem

i don't need someone to teach me how to work out. i just need a reason to go back.

most people who stop going to the gym don't quit because they gave up on fitness. something interrupted — a new job, an injury, a rough few months — and once the habit broke, going back started feeling bigger than it actually was.

existing apps are built for people who are already consistent. they track progress, assign programs, and measure performance. none of them are built for the moment when someone just needs to walk back in without it feeling like a big deal.

77%

of fitness app users stop opening the app within 3 days.

50%

of new gym members quit within 6 months.

0

mainstream apps designed for the person coming back, not starting fresh.

who i designed for

the comeback user, 28–42 yrs

previously consistent for 3–6 months. stopped due to injury, job change, or burnout. they still have a gym membership. they know what a deadlift is. what they don't have is a way back in that doesn't feel like admitting something.

the core insight

i need something that makes going feel smaller than not going.

where the product came from

the first version of this idea was a workout generator where you pick a muscle group and get exercises. when i tested it, people said it solved the wrong thing. boredom wasn't keeping them away from the gym. the feeling of starting over was.

that changed how i thought about the product. the design problem wasn't about content or features. it was about how the app made someone feel the moment they opened it after weeks away.

our principle

we reduce guilt, not awareness. every session ends with the user knowing they did something real today, and that there is more to build on next time.

the product

every element of the interface is designed to answer a single question: how do we make a person who hasn't been to the gym in four months feel capable of being there today?

comeback entry
the first thing you see when you open the app.

the screen asks two things: how are you feeling, and how much time do you have. that is all it needs to give you something useful. everything else — equipment, fitness level, goals — is in the profile and is set up once.

the suggestion
one thing to do today. that's it.

you cannot browse categories or pick something different from a list. there is one thing to do today, and a quiet link that says "show me something easier" if you need it.

active session
the timer is the only thing on screen.

the full dark background marks a shift - you are not browsing anymore, you are doing something. your current activity and what comes next is all that shows.

completion
the headline is always "you showed up today."

the session strip shows minutes completed and a small upward arrow. there is no next workout suggestion, no streak notification, and no share button.

what i learned
a note on restraint

the main work on this project was removing things. each time i added something, i asked whether a person who hadn't been to the gym in months would close the app when they saw it. that question shaped almost every decision.
