overview

every year, hundreds of thousands of indian students move to a new city for college. they arrive with ambition, a packed bag, and no one who knows their name.

signals is a mobile app for those first 90 days. it combines structured daily challenges with an anonymous campus feed, giving students a low-stakes way to act, and a real-time way to know they are not the only one struggling.

the problem

nobody told their parents how they actually felt. they called home and said everything was fine.

59.9%

of indian college students show signs of depression in year one across 8 tier-1 cities.

45.5%

study outside their home state, a new city, zero social infrastructure.

0

dedicated products for indian college student loneliness exist in the market today.

the wound isn't the absence of people. it's the belief that you're the only one feeling their absence.

decisions that mattered

every element on every screen has a reason. these are the four that changed the product most.

01
value before sign-up

the first challenge appears before account creation - by the time sign-up shows up, aarav already has something to protect.

endowment effect - people overvalue what they already have.
02
hollow dots, not x marks

a missed day stays hollow, never crossed out - because one pixel difference is the difference between "i'm still going" and "i've already failed."

zeigarnik effect - incomplete arcs pull you forward. broken ones push you away.
03
anonymous by default

he won't say he's lonely. but he'll tap "me too" on someone else saying it. five steps from anonymous reading to name reveal - the design earns each one before asking for the next.

progressive disclosure - only ask for what trust has been built for.
04
the pre-filled first message

the blank box after "say hi" is where most people close the app. signals writes the first line. editable, but most people send it as is.

default effect - the best default is the one that makes doing nothing harder than doing something.
what i learned
a note on tone

to find the right words for the users. with every screen, every label, every cta, i had to design for a student who would close the app the moment it made him feel defined by his struggle. the product had to say "you're not alone" without ever saying "you're lonely." that constraint shaped everything.

what comes next

signals is designed for the first 90 days. what happens after week 12 is intentionally unanswered - because by then, the app should no longer be the most important relationship in a student's life.

V2 explores city mapping, group challenges, and a senior mentor layer. but those come after one student has helped one other student find a laundromat, or sat somewhere new for lunch, or sent a message that started with "i feel that too."
